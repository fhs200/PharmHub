Metformin

Purpose

Metformin helps control blood sugar levels in people with Type 2 Diabetes. It’s often the first-line treatment to help manage blood glucose, especially when combined with diet and exercise.

How It Works:

Metformin works by reducing the amount of glucose the liver produces and enhancing the body’s response to insulin, which helps cells absorb glucose more effectively. It does not increase insulin levels directly, making it less likely to cause low blood sugar (hypoglycemia).

Common Side Effects

Nausea or upset stomach: Taking metformin with food may help reduce nausea.

Diarrhea: This often improves over time as the body adjusts to the medication.

Metallic taste: Usually mild and may go away after continued use.

Management: If side effects are persistent, consult a healthcare provider. Starting at a lower dose may also help the body adjust gradually.

For minor side effects, try a lower dose or take it with food. If symptoms worsen, consult a healthcare provider.

Potential Drug Interactions:

  • Diuretics (e.g., furosemide): May increase the risk of lactic acidosis.
  • Iodine Contrast Media: Used in imaging procedures, it may increase the risk of kidney problems; metformin may need to be stopped before imaging.
  • Alcohol: Can increase the risk of lactic acidosis, a rare but serious side effect.
