Importance of Adherence

Improves Treatment Effectiveness

Sticking to the specified medication regimen increases the drug's effectiveness and helps achieve the targeted therapeutic effects. Without regular adherence, the medication may not operate as intended, resulting in unsatisfactory outcomes.

Reduces Disease Complications

Adherence to treatment can slow the evolution of diseases and lower the chance of complications. Adherence is crucial for chronic illnesses such as diabetes and hypertension in order to maintain control and avoid long-term damage

Lowers Healthcare Costs

When patients follow their drug regimen, they are less likely to require emergency care or hospitalisation. This not only benefits the patient by increasing their quality of life, but it also lowers overall healthcare expenses for both individuals and the healthcare system.

Prevents Antibiotic Resistance

In the case of antibiotics, failure to complete the entire course can contribute to antibiotic resistance, making subsequent infections more difficult to treat. Completing the suggested course is required to completely eradicate the illness.

Improves Quality of Life

Medication adherence can reduce symptoms and enhance daily functioning, especially for chronic diseases. Patients who keep to their pharmaceutical regimen generally report improved physical and mental health.
