Ibuprofen

Purpose

Ibuprofen is commonly used to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and bring down fever. It’s prescribed for headaches, muscle pain, arthritis, menstrual cramps, and minor injuries.

How It Works

Ibuprofen works by inhibiting the enzymes (COX-1 and COX-2) responsible for producing prostaglandins, substances that contribute to inflammation, pain, and fever.

Common Side Effects

  • Stomach Pain or Discomfort: Taking ibuprofen with food or milk can help reduce this effect.
  • Nausea: Staying hydrated and avoiding alcohol may help manage nausea.
  • Dizziness: Refrain from driving or operating heavy machinery if dizziness occurs.
  • Heartburn: Taking ibuprofen with food or an antacid can help prevent heartburn.

For minor side effects, try a lower dose or take it with food. If symptoms worsen, consult a healthcare provider.

Potential Drug Interactions:

  • Blood thinners (e.g., warfarin): Ibuprofen can increase the risk of bleeding.
  • Other NSAIDs (e.g., aspirin): Increases the risk of gastrointestinal issues.
  • Antihypertensive medications (e.g., ACE inhibitors): Ibuprofen may reduce the effectiveness of blood pressure medications.
  • Lithium: Ibuprofen may increase lithium levels in the blood, leading to toxicity.
