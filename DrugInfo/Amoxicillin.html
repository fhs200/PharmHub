Amoxicillin

Purpose:

Amoxicillin is an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections such as respiratory tract infections, ear infections, urinary tract infections, and certain types of skin infections.

How It Works:

Amoxicillin works by interfering with the cell wall synthesis of bacteria, which weakens the bacteria's cell wall and eventually kills it. It belongs to the penicillin class of antibiotics and is effective against a broad range of bacteria.

Common Side Effects

Nausea: Take with food to reduce this effect.

Diarrhea: Drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated.

Rash: Notify a healthcare provider if a rash appears.

Management: Side effects are often mild and resolve on their own. However, if symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare provider.

Potential Drug Interactions:

  • Methotrexate: Amoxicillin may increase methotrexate levels, raising the risk of toxicity.
  • Oral Contraceptives: Amoxicillin may reduce the effectiveness of birth control pills; using additional contraception is recommended.
  • Allopurinol: Increases the likelihood of a skin rash when taken with amoxicillin.
